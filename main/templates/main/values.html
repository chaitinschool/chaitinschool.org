Inclusivity

One of the problematic parts of the current state of universities is their exclusivity. Whether it is tuition fees, applications, acceptance criteria, location, or passport it is a mechanism that prevents people from learning.

We stand against this in practice. We welcome everybody who wants to take part in our activities.

The only caveat is our Code of Conduct, which is hopefully appropriate and welcomed by everybody.

Expertise

We view software building not only as engineering but also as a craft. Crafts are associated with skill. There is also an element of art in a craft.

We value both skill and creativity. Both are things we want to develop. Among others, these require practice, intelligence, consistency, and persistence.

Fun

We venture into computers because they are fun. This element of enjoyment is fundamental to our approach.

Humour

Why is something funny? Humour is such a unique quality that it’s unexpectedly hard to define. It’s also fundamental to programmers, from naming things to April Fool’s jokes.

We also don’t want to take ourselves too seriously.

Gift economy

I watch how the moon sits in the sky in the dark night
Shining with the light from the sun
And the sun doesn't give light to the moon assuming
The moon's gonna owe it one
— Linkin Park

There are no transactions involved in what we do. We gift as much as we feel comfortable. What usually happens is that people appreciate it and gift themselves too. But it’s neither required nor expected.

No knowledge authorities

Universities work in a model where people usually depend on a professor authority to provide absolution. This is definitely useful (having someone with experience on the matter who knows the answers), however the innate structural hierarchy of universities means that people are constrained by the power of one.

The validity of the statement is more evidently confirmed when speaking to someone in a higher academic level such as MSc or PhD.

Discovered and undiscovered knowledge

Men learn while they teach.
— Seneca the Younger

We are interested in the knowledge behind computer science and software engineering. Whether some of it has already been discoevered is irrelevant. We are interested in both kinds—and most times, by thinking about what you already know, you discover what you don’t.

Community

Happiness is only real when shared.
— Jon Krakauer

Chaitin School as a project has started as the vision of one. However, there is only value to it if it’s shared. That’s one of the hard parts. Not because there aren’t many great people to share it with; but only because it’s hard to define what it is.
